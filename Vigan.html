Vigan City

Vigan, a 4th-class component city in Ilocos Sur, Philippines, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the few Spanish colonial towns in the country. With a population of 53,935 people, it is located on the western coast of Luzon Island and faces the South China Sea. Vigan is known for its unique architecture, blending Native Philippine and Oriental building designs with colonial Spanish architecture, including bahay na bato houses and an Earthquake Baroque church. Former Philippine president Elpidio Quirino was born in Vigan, and he resided in the Syquia Mansion, a wedding gift from his in-laws. The city was inscribed as a UNESCO World Heritage City in 2015 and is a member of the Organization of World Heritage Cities (OWHC). In May 2015, Vigan was recognized as one of the New7Wonders Cities, alongside Beirut, Doha, Durban, Havana, Kuala Lumpur, and La Paz.

History

Vigan was a significant coastal trading post in pre-colonial times, where Chinese junks sailed from the South China Sea to Isla de Bigan. Sea-faring merchants bartered exotic goods from Asian kingdoms in exchange for gold, beeswax, and mountain products from the Cordilleras. Immigrants, mostly Chinese, settled in Vigan, intermarried with the natives, and started the multi-cultural bloodline of the Bigue√±os. In 1572, King Philip II sent Captain Juan de Salcedo with around 80 soldiers to explore the coast of Los Ilocano. After a successful expedition, Don Juan de Salcedo founded "Villa Fernandina de Vigan" in honor of King Philip II's son, Prince Ferdinand, who died at the tender age of four. Salcedo was awarded the old province of Ylocos as his Encomienda and was given the title as Justicia Mayor de esta Provincia de Ylocos. In January 1574, Salcedo returned to Vigan, bringing Augustinian Missionaries to pioneer the evangelization of Ylocos and establish a Spanish city to control neighboring countries. The place was named Bigan after a native mistakenly believed that Salcedo was asking for the name of a tuber plant from the "gabi" or taro family.
